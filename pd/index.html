Cryptographic Signet Ring

01 — The Problem

"Your mother gets a call.
It's your voice.
She can't tell it's not you."

AI can clone a voice in three seconds. Replicate a face from a photograph. Forge a signature from a sample. Digital identity is becoming unreliable.

02 — The Insight

You can't steal
possession remotely.

Information leaks. Objects don't. For four thousand years, a signet ring pressed into wax was the most trusted form of identity.

Physical presence is the last unforgeable signal.

03 — The Solution

The modern
signet.

A precision-machined brass antenna with an embedded cryptographic chip. No battery. No screen. No expiry. The grooves on the ring face are the antenna. The antenna is the circuit. Nothing is hidden.

04 — How It Works

Tap to sign.

Hold the ring to any NFC-enabled phone. The phone powers the chip. The chip generates a unique cryptographic signature — verifiable on-chain via LUKSO.

Proof you were there. No app. No pairing. Just tap.

05 — The Network

constellations.
